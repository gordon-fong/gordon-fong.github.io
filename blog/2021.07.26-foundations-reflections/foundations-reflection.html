July 26, 2021

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

I have learned that I really enjoy sharing and expanding my knowledge with others. A few times during our javascript sections, I think I have aided others in their understanding and development of breaking down problems. When I help, I exercise the listening and looping aspect along with emotional intelligence to understand their approach and how they feel about it so we can pursue this path together.

In turn, I feel this has improved my confidence and allowed me to practice my communication skills, in terms of explaining ideas and concepts in simple ways. And through this, I have also learned that I really like analogies! I found that it's a great way to learn.

I realised that a lot of the time in the past, I had a fixed mindset. This often came from seeing great art and I would think “Wow, this looks really cool. I wish I could do that.” Being aware of neuroplasticity and making an effort to maintain a growth mindset has given me a lot of motivation to take small steps towards accomplishing big goals.


What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

I think all of these are most important when working in a team environment. The values we bring contribute to what we can offer to the team. Empathy us to understand how the team is feeling / thinking so everyone works better together. Self awareness of our own strengths and limitations help keep us on task and shapes how we might approach a problem. Being self aware of our own fatigue and emotional balance is also important to take proper care of ourselves.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

A couple things. How in depth the learning goes and how much I struggled with it. I think it has helped the most with making me aware of where and how to get my motivation. Something that will be very important in bootcamp, I’m sure.


What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I have struggled the most with putting my thoughts into words and really diving deep as to how I feel about certain aspects.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

To prepare us for employment. The fact that employers view the hard/technical skills second to being a good person, has popped up a few times. The technical training can be learned on the job, but being someone that people want to work with is what gets people jobs in general.


Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

The time spent on core was worth it. I have been spending what feels like the most time on these sections. I think they are important to learn to be aware of and a good change of pace away from code.
