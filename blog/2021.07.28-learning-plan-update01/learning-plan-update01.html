July 28, 2021

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I think my ultimate goal would be to get into game development. I believe that is where my skill set and way of thinking can be the most effective. I realize that this is an industry that will be difficult to find a position in, but is pursuable as a hobby too (until a job opens up!).

Front-end development is still up there in terms of what I enjoy coding the most (so far!). I have an appreciation of UI and UX design. Though I only have limited knowledge of it at this point, I would like to explore this area further.


What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I’m managing my perfectionist habit differently. I’m quite selective now in what I decide to spend my time on. Our current website, tech blogs, and minesweeper were things that I have chosen to spend the most time on so far. I set myself a time limit on any extras and only really work on them whenever I have time to spare.

Momentum is still there, but so are the distractions! Though the ‘distractions’ have become more constructive and relevant to learning :D


What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Speaking in front of the group. I’m not great at public speaking and my nerves usually make me trip and stumble over my words. This may be less of a problem than earlier though, as I’ve now had the privilege of meeting and getting to know about half the cohort over the last few weeks in foundations.


What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I definitely want to develop my communication and leadership skills. I think these are the two I lack the most of. I think I give and receive feedback okay, but there is room for improvement.


What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

To be there when help is needed and to guide us in case we stray off the track. I’ll be making these efforts myself as well, but I may not always succeed.


What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I hope to grow in the non-technical aspects. It will ultimately be up to myself to step up when the time comes to take advantage of these opportunities. I reckon that maintaining a growth mindset and being open to new experiences are what will get me through and get the most out of bootcamp.

For managing the workload, I need to write up realistic timeboxes, stay within them and stay on task (when not on a break!). I am always open to helping and will learn to voice my own struggles earlier so I don’t fall behind and get overwhelmed.
