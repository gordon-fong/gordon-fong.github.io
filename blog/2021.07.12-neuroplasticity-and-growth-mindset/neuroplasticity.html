Neuroplasticity

-

How does understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefit people?

When people learn how their brain works, they become more motivated and open to learning, knowing that everyone possesses the ability to learn.

Being actively aware of how you learn benefits the learning process.

It is also something that aids in the recovery after accidents, or to overcome disabilities.


How might I engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my own benefit?

Doing these reflections is a very effective way of engaging with neuroplasticity. Reflecting on new material allows me to traverse the pathways I have just made and strengthen them, allowing me to digest the information more effectively.


What are some of the ways to increase my neuroplasticity?

Practice what I have learnt over the day.

Think about how I could apply the new information into something practical, or into a personal project going forward.

Growth Mindset

-

What is it and why is it relevant?

It’s a way of thinking. To understand and accept that we may not know everything, but we all possess the ability to grow and learn new things to become better.

Everyone is unique in their way of learning. It is important to understand what is most effective for you.

It’s oozing with positive VIBES and motivation!


Did anything surprise or change for me?

Prior to knowing about the two mindsets, I think there are times where I would fall into the fixed category. I will make active efforts to change my way of thinking until it becomes natural.

I found it to be a positive and inspirational topic and fuel for motivation.


How will I integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey?

One aspect of Carol Deck’s Ted Talk that I found fascinating was her example of the power of the word “yet”. It leaves a pathway open to success. I know that there will be aspects to this course that I will struggle in, but I will choose to embrace the challenge and persevere through obstacles, knowing that I will get there in my own time.
